    • Arkansas Baptist College

    Baptist (Consolidated Missionary Baptist State Convention of Arkansas) HBCU

    "About Us" page has a link to Strategic Plan with explicitly Christian aims under "Spiritual Vitality" (https://www.arkansasbaptist.edu/wp-content/uploads/2020/06/ABC_Strategic_Plan_2023_062020.pdf)

    "As one of Arkansas’ most affordable institutions of higher education, the College strives to attract students who desire a personal approach to their education with smaller classes, dedicated instructors, a sense of community, and spiritual values and principles integrated throughout their collegiate experience. Arkansas Baptist College, originally named the Minister’s Institute, was founded in 1884 by the Colored Baptists of Arkansas during their annual convention at Mount Zion Baptist Church in Little Rock." (https://www.arkansasbaptist.edu)

    Christian identity confirmed: "Arkansas Baptist College prepares students for a life of service grounded in academic scholarship, the liberal arts tradition, social responsibility, Christian Development and preparation for employment in a global community."
